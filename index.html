Overview

Background

The Modern Day Second American Civil War represents the complete collapse and fragmentation of the United States into 10 independent factions. Unlike traditional civil wars with clear sides, this conflict features multiple independent actors with no formal alliances. Each faction operates autonomously with its own government, military, and territorial control. The situation is characterized by:

  • No formal alliances between any factions
  • Each faction acts independently in its own interests
  • Primary rivalry between Republican and Democratic successor states
  • Multiple independent republics pursuing separate agendas
  • Neutral zones controlled by military or anarchist forces
  • Complex multi-polar conflict with shifting dynamics

Causes

The collapse of the United States and subsequent civil war emerged from specific triggering events:

  • Early 2025 LA Riots: Anti-I.C.E. riots in Los Angeles triggered California's secession
  • Texas Independence Movement: Long-standing independence groups successfully broke Texas away
  • Political Polarization: Complete breakdown between Republican and Democratic parties
  • Regional Civil Unrest: Widespread social disorder in New England and Cascadia
  • Native American Sovereignty: Indigenous nations asserting independence and equality
  • Southern Nationalism: Revival of Confederate ideology in southern states
  • Social Collapse: Complete breakdown of order in Alaska leading to anarchy
