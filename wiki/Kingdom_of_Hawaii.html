History of the Restored Kingdom

Historical Context (1893-2025)

The original Kingdom of Hawaii was illegally overthrown in 1893 by American businessmen, leading to annexation by the United States in 1898. For over a century, the Native Hawaiian sovereignty movement worked to restore independence and traditional governance.

The Collapse and Opportunity (December 2025)

As the United States dissolved in the final days of 2025, Hawaii found itself abandoned by the federal government. The state government, recognizing the historical illegitimacy of American control, organized a restoration referendum.

"This is not secession - this is the restoration of a sovereign nation that was never legally incorporated into the United States." - Queen Lili ªuokalani II, Coronation Address

The Restoration Referendum (December 30, 2025)

In an emergency referendum held on December 30, 2025:

  • 78% voted for restoration of the Hawaiian Kingdom
  • 62% of Native Hawaiians participated in the vote
  • 89% of Native Hawaiian voters supported restoration
  • Immigration policy was established to respect existing residents

Early Restoration Period (2026)

The new kingdom immediately focused on:

  • Establishing diplomatic relations with Pacific nations
  • Revitalizing Hawaiian language and culture
  • Reforming land ownership based on traditional Hawaiian concepts
  • Building a constitutional monarchy with modern democratic elements
  • Securing the islands against potential reconquest attempts

International Recognition (2026-Present)

The Kingdom quickly gained recognition from:

  • Japan, Australia, and New Zealand (first to recognize)
  • Pacific Island nations including Fiji, Samoa, and Tonga
  • Indigenous sovereignty movements worldwide
  • Several European nations seeking Pacific trade partners
