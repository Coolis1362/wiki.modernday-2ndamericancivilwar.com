History of Naval Command

Pre-Collapse Military Structure (Before December 2025)

The US Navy was the world's largest and most technologically advanced naval force, with global reach and strategic responsibilities including:

  • Nuclear deterrent through submarine-launched ballistic missiles
  • Power projection via aircraft carrier strike groups
  • Global maritime security and freedom of navigation operations
  • Strategic overseas bases and alliance commitments

The Emergency Declaration (December 28, 2025)

As the federal government collapsed, Admiral Patricia Morrison, then Chief of Naval Operations, issued Emergency Order 2025-001:

  • Declared martial law in all naval installations and surrounding areas
  • Assumed command of all naval forces under wartime emergency protocols
  • Established the Naval High Command as interim government
  • Initiated "Operation Poseidon" to secure critical naval assets
"The United States Navy will not abandon its duty to protect American interests and maintain freedom of the seas, regardless of political upheaval on land." - Fleet Admiral Patricia Morrison, Emergency Declaration

Securing the Fleet (December 29-31, 2025)

In the final days of 2025, Naval Command executed rapid consolidation:

  • East Coast: Secured Norfolk Naval Base, Navy Yard Portsmouth, and Naval Station Mayport
  • West Coast: Maintained control of Naval Base San Diego and portions of Naval Base Coronado
  • Strategic Assets: Secured nuclear submarine bases at Kings Bay, GA and Bangor, WA
  • Overseas: Maintained control of Guantanamo Bay, Diego Garcia, and select Pacific installations

Early Consolidation Period (2026)

The Naval Command focused on:

  • Establishing civilian administration in controlled territories
  • Negotiating ceasefires with emerging factions
  • Securing nuclear weapons and preventing proliferation
  • Maintaining operational readiness of the fleet
  • Protecting American citizens in controlled areas

Current Operations (2026-Present)

Naval Command continues to operate as a military government while:

  • Patrolling international waters to maintain US maritime presence
  • Protecting remaining American territories and overseas interests
  • Conducting humanitarian operations for displaced civilians
  • Maintaining deterrent capabilities against foreign threats
  • Seeking diplomatic recognition as legitimate US successor
