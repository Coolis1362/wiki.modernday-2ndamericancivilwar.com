History of Independence

The Path to Secession

California's break from the United States was triggered by specific events in early 2025, culminating in a declaration of independence that established the 2nd California Republic.

January 2025

Rising Tensions

Federal immigration policies intensify, leading to increased I.C.E. operations in California cities.

February 2025

LA Riots Begin

Anti-I.C.E. riots erupt in Los Angeles following controversial federal enforcement actions. Protests spread statewide.

March 2025

State Resistance

California state government refuses federal orders, declares sanctuary status for entire state.

December 27, 2025

California Breaks Away

2nd California Republic declares independence, triggering nationwide succession crisis.

December 28, 2025

Union of America Forms

Democratic states declare independence as Union of America, escalating the constitutional crisis.

December 29, 2025

Southern Independence Declaration

USSA and Hopi Nation simultaneously declare independence, claiming constitutional legitimacy and sovereignty rights.

November 30, 2025

Republican Union Formation

Trump flees to Midwest US and forms Republican Union of America as conservative counter-government.

December 30, 2025

Regional Breakaways

Hawaii, Texas, New England, and Cascadia declare independence in rapid succession.

December 31, 2025

Federal Collapse

Alaska's social order collapses into anarchy, US Navy flees to territories. United States officially ceases to exist.

January 1, 2026

Second American Civil War Begins

With federal authority completely collapsed, the Second American Civil War officially commences between successor states.

The 1st California Republic Legacy

The name "2nd California Republic" honors the short-lived California Republic of 1846, which lasted 25 days during the Bear Flag Revolt. Key connections include:

  • Adoption of the Bear Flag as the national symbol
  • Republican form of government
  • Independent spirit and self-determination
  • Resistance to external control
